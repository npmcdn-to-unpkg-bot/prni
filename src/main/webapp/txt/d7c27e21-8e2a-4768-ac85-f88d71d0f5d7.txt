  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… The Secretariat of the Pacific Islands Applied Geoscience Commission conducted a high-resolution bathymetric mapping survey of the seabed surrounding the island of Niue. ¶ Survey resulted in the acquisition of over 550 km of multibeam echosounder (MBES) data. A temporary tide gauge was also installed at the Alofi Wharf for the duration of the survey. ¶ The survey achieved 100 % coverage of the seafloor from approximately 50 m depth in then nearshore area to an average offshore distance of 5 km, reaching water depths of some 2000 m. Resultant data compilation was used to produce bathymetry charts. The datasets in ascii, tab delimiter, xyz files are available in this metadata entry and the charts are available as separate metadata entries here on the SOPAC Geonetwork site. ¶ This work was initiated by the SOPAC/EU project ‘Reducing Vulnerability of Pacific ACP ¶ States’.

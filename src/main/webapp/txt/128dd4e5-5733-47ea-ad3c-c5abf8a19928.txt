  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………… These 6 photosets are collections of historical black and white (panchromatic) aerial photographs that was taken around the Pohnpei Island in the Federated States of Micronesia, acquired in 1975. Coverage is limited to areas that fall around the Northern tip of Deke Sokehs, channel area between Takaieu and Maramosok, Sokeh's Harbour, channel area between Temwen Island and Tamworohi, coastal and reef area of Mudokolos in the eastern part of Pohnpei and areas around Southern coastal area of Rohi. ¶ The original paper prints have been scanned at a resolution of 1,200 DPI on a Magnum G600 Wide format scanner and stored as TIFF format images. All 14 Photographs have not been rectified but mosaicked for viewing purposes and uploaded according to their frame numbers. These photosets are held at the Secretariat on behalf of the FSM Goverment.
  │   ├─Purpose………………………………………………………………………………… The Secretariats aerial photographs is a unique detailed record of the physical landscapes from the different member Pacific Island countries. These photographs can be used for diverse purposes by government agencies, surveyors, planners, consulting scientists, engineers and other individuals. These purposes include determination of past land uses, marine mapping, coral reef habitat mapping ( change detection), restoration of natural areas, assessing historical changes , and a variety of other applications.

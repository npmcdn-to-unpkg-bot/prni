  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This record holds a Navigation Map, Sheet 7 of Melanesian Arc Gap Area in Solomon Islands. The map is part of the SOPACMAPS [Project Sheets 1-25] collection produced for the SOPACMAPS Project Leg 2 cruise that was conducted by the oceanographical research vessel L'Atalante in September 9, 1993. Prior to the at-sea operations, a sheet assemblage (at 1/250,000 scale) was established and used during the survey and for the processing of the first set of charts. The swath mapping project was funded by the EEC (Lome III - European Development Fund No 6) known as SOPACMAPS, and was awarded to IFREMER. A copy of the map can be downloaded by selecting the appropriate link under 'Online resources' from this record with permission from the Secretariat.

The Solomon Islands Arc ends abruptly northeast of San Cristobal Island. The far north of the New Hebrides Island Arc is also sharply interrupted northwards and westwards at the Reef Islands and Tinakula volcano. Between those two arcs, lies a basin with an average depth of 4,000 m, apparently unnamed, although its western part is occasionaly named Ulawa Deep, from the name of the Ulawa Island, north of San Cristobal. We propose naming this region the "Melanesian Arc Gap"

  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This record holds the ASCII Tab delimited bathymetry dataset of Arutanga Passage ,Rarotonga . Dataset was collected using an Echotrac single beam 200-kHz sounder with DGPS for navigation, control based on UTM Zone 4 WGS84 Spheroid. For tide corrections, data retrieved from the gauge sited in Avatiu Harbour was used to reduce all soundings for the harbours surveyed on Rarotonga. All data has been reduced to tide gauge zero with is reported to be 0.02 m above Lowest Astronomical Tide 92-93 as established by the National Tidal Facility Project. These bathymetry dataset are held at the SOPAC Secretariat on behalf of the Cook Islands Goverment. Dataset can be downloaded from the appropriate link under Distribution Infor with appropriate permission from the Secretariat. ¶  ¶  ¶  ¶ Bathymetric maps of the four harbours in the Cook Islands,  For the Arutanga Harbour, Aitutaki, tidal reduction of soundings was based on measured data from a triton water level gauge.
  │   ├─Purpose………………………………………………………………………………………… The secretariats bathymetry dataset is a unique detailed record of physical underwater landscape of the different member Pacific Island countries. These bathymetry can be used for diverse purposes by government agencies, surveyors, planners, consulting scientists, navigators, and other individuals. These purposes include the study of rocks and minerals on the ocean floor, mineral exploration, study of underwater earthquakes or volcanoes, diving missions, paleobathymetry, monitoring of ecology of an ocean bed, assessment of the possible impact of hydrodynamic changes in rivers, lakes and determining of suitability of channels for barge passage. Bathymetry is a crucial component in many fields of marine research and hydrology

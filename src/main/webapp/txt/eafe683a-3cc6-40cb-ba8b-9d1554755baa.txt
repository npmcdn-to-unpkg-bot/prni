  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This metadata entry holds a gridded bathymetry data of Tavua Bay, Fiji Islands collected in 1995. Original dataset are stored in Universal Traverse Mercator (UTM) WGS84 which are referenced to MapInfo standard projection. Bathymetry datasets are available for download from the Distribution section of this entry as ,ASCII files, MapInfo files and as Shapefiles, with appropriate permission from the Secretariat. Datasets also include contour file of the area in .dxf file format, MapInfo and as shapefiles. ¶  ¶  ¶ Survey was conducted by the South Pacific Applied Geoscience Commission (SOPAC) and the Mineral Resources Department, Fiji (MRD) between the 5th and the 15th December in 1995, to fulfil the requirements of Task Survey ID FJ9501 - FJ11. The survey was funded principally by the Government of the Republic of Fiji with additional ¶ support from the European Union and the Governments of Canada and Australia.

Data obtained from Geodetic Section, Lands & Survey Department. ¶  ¶ The odom echotrac precision echo sounder provided both analogue and digital records of ¶ bathymetric information within the area of investigation. Digital bathymetry data was logged ¶ and processed on the Del Norte 1009 microwave/GPS digital distance measuring unit, and ¶ analogue position and depth data on an EPG graphic recorder. The bar check method was used to calibrate the echo sounder. All bathymetric data acquired during the survey were reduced to chart datum based on tidal information extracted from the Lautoka tide gauge for the final report of results.
  │       └─Process step                         
  │           └─Description………………………………………………………… BATHYMETRY COLLECTION PROCESS:  An adom echotrac precision echo sounder provided both analogue and digital records of bathymetric information within the area of investigation. Digital bathymetry data was logged and processed on the Del Norte 1009 microwave/GPS digital distance measuring unit, and analogue position and depth data on an EPG graphic recorder. The bar check method was used to calibrate the echo sounder. All bathymetric data acquired during the survey were reduced to chart datum based on tidal information extracted from the Lautoka tide gauge for the final report of results.

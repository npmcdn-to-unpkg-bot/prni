  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… The Pacific Islands Applied Geoscience Commission (SOPAC) carried out a marine survey for Tuvalu in the waters around all nine atolls and low reef islands, namely, Nanumea, ¶ Niutao, Nanumanga, Nui, Vaitupu, Nukufetau, Funafuti, Nukulaelae, Niulakita.  This work was initiated by the SOPAC/EU Reducing Vulnerability of Pacific ACP States Project. ¶ This report describes the high-resolution bathymetric mapping survey carried out over a ¶ period of five weeks in September and October 2004. The survey achieved good coverage of the seafloor from approximately 10 m depth in the nearshore reef slope area, to an average offshore depth of some 2000 m, at an average slope angle of 27°. ¶ The resultant data was used to produce nine bathymetry charts for Tuvalu at scales ranging ¶ from 1 : 20 000 to 1 : 50 000 available as separate metadata entry, under the different surveyed islands. The report is available for download from the Distribution Info section.

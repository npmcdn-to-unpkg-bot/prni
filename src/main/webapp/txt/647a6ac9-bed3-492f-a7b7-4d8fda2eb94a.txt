  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This metadata entry holds a gridded bathymetry data of Nadi Bay, Fiji Islands in 1994. Original dataset are stored in Universal Traverse Mercator (UTM) WGS84 which are referenced to MapInfo standard projection. Datasets are available for download from the Distribution section of this entry as MapInfo files, ASCII tab delimited files and as Shapefiles, with appropriate permission from the Secretariat. Bathymetry map also include contour file of the area in .dxf file and in ESRII shapefiles. ¶  ¶ This metadata entry holds a gridded bathymetry data of Nadi Bay, Fiji Islands, 1994. Original dataset are stored in Universal Traverse Mercator (UTM) WGS84 which are referenced to MapInfo standard projection. Bathymetry datasets are available for download from the Distribution section of this entry as MapInfo formats, ASCII tab delimited files and as Shapefiles, with appropriate permission from the Secretariat. The contour dataset of the area in .dxf ,shapefiles and MapInfo also available for download with the survey report. Survey was conducted by the South Pacific Applied Geoscience Commission (SOPAC) and the Mineral Resources Department, Fiji (MRD) between 5 and 20 December, 1996, to fulfill the requirements of Task FJ 9603. Bathymetry data was gathered as part of the preliminary results of a field survey to assess the potential of detrital gold in areas in and adjacent to Momi Bay west Viti Levu, Fiji. Approximately 45 line kilometres of data was acquired. This survey was funded principally by the Government of the Republic of Fiji with additional support from the Government of Australia and the European Union.

Because it separates different sedimentary environments, there ¶ is very gentle but continuous deepening of water depths in the southern bay compared to the northern bay. ¶ Water depths reach 20 m approximately 6 km from the mouth of the Vuda River. Except for the topographic ¶ high which cuts the bay, Nadi Bay is an archetypal bay showing gentle increasing of water depths towards ¶ the sea. ¶ The mouths of the Vuda River, Sabeto River, Nasoso River and Nadi River are river-dominated deltas with ¶ low wave energy and low littoral drift, except for the Nadi River delta which appears to be have higher ¶ energy and stronger littoral drift, as indicated by lateral displacement of the delta lobe to the west of the river ¶ mouth.

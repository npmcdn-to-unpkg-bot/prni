  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… The Pacific Islands Applied Geoscience Commission (SOPAC) carried out a marine survey for Majuro Atoll in the Marshall Islands. High-resolution bathymetric survey carried over a period of 10 days from 18 July to 27 July, resulting in the acquisition of over 751.858 km of Multibeam echosounder data. ¶ The survey achieved 100% coverage of the seafloor in the lagoon from approximately 10 to 70 m water depths in the lagoon and approximately 10 m depth nearshore area to an average offshore distance of 5 km, reaching water depths of some 2 200 m. The resultant data compilation was used to produce bathymetry charts of Majuro at a scale of 1:50 000. The compiled bathymetry dataset reveals the main morphological features of the seabed in the lagoon and also around the eastern offshore areas. ¶ The dataset in ascii, tab delimiter, xyz files are available from this metadata entry for download. The charts and related reports can be found here as a separate entry on the SOPAC Geonetwork site.

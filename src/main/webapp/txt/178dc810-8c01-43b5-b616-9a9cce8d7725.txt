  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This record holds a bathymetry map for Iron Bottom Sound, Central Solomon Trough (CST) in the Solomon Islands. The map is drawn on a 10 m contour interval and Sounding Equipment: Simrad EM12 Dual. The map is a working copy and is part of a new sheet assemblage adopted for the final results that was produced from reprocessing of the data by IFREMER during the SOPACMAPS Project Leg 2. The SOPACMAPS Project Leg 2 was funded by the European Community and conducted by the oceanographical research vessel L'Atalante from 19 July 1993 to 20 October 1993. The map shows the area between Guadalcanal, Florida and the Savo Islands. A copy of the map can also be downloaded by selecting the appropriate link under 'Online resources' with permission from the Secretariat.

The SOPACMAPS Project was designed to investigate as much area as possible of the EEZs of the SOPAC member countries: Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, Fiji and Tuvalu.The main objectives were to map the bathymetry, investigate the resource potential and to study geological processes, including geological hazards. The 85 m R/V L'Atalante, launched in 1990, was built specifically for swath mapping surveys and carries a dual Simrad EM12 multibeam bathymetric system. The schedule of the cruises was: * Leg 1- 19 July in Noumea (New Caledonia) to 15 August 1993 in Honiara (Solomon Islands), * Leg 2 - 19 August in Honiara (Solomon Islands) to 16 September 1993 in Suva (Fiji), * Leg 3 - 22 September in Suva (Fiji) to 20 October 1993 in Noumea (New Caledonia). The Melanesian Arc Gap Area was, before the SOPACMAPS cruise, poorly mapped, and only few information were available. That region had not been surveyed during the Tripartite 1982-1984 program.

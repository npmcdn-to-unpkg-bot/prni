  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… The Pacific Islands Applied Geoscience Commission, SOPAC, carried out a marine survey for Papua New Guinea around, Madang, Sissano and Vanimo. High-resolution bathymetric mapping survey was carried out over a period of 5 weeks in September and October 2005, resulting in the acquisition of over 733 line-km of MBES data. The survey achieved variable coverage of 50-100% (less for shallow water areas) of the seafloor from approximately 50 m in the inshore area to an average offshore distance of 4 km, reaching maximum water depths of some 2000 m.The resultant data was used to produce bathymetry charts and is available for download from its separate individual metadata entry. The text delimited XYZ files are available from here for download. ¶ This work was initiated by the Pacific Islands Applied Geoscience Commission (SOPAC) and the European Union (EU) Reducing Vulnerability of Pacific ACP States Project.

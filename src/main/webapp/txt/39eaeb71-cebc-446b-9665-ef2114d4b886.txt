  │   ├─Abstract……………………………………………………………………………………… This record holds an ASCII Tab delimited bathymetry dataset of Anearshore area from Avarua to Avatiu harbour . ¶ This operation was undertaken for the CCOP/SOPAC Work Programme CCSP/CK.8 "Feasibility studies for Harnessing Wave Energy for Rarotonga funded by the Goverment of Norway. As part of a wave energy study program in the South Pacific the Norwegian Goverment also supplied CCOP/SOPAC with Waverider Buoys for installation in several locations in the CCOP/SOPAC area . The result of the survey are datasets of the wave rider buoy (wave site) area and the Avarua to Avatiu harbour offshore dataset. that was extracted from a complete rarotonga nearshore mutibeam bathymetry survey which was done for the Australian goverment's overseas aid program and further processed for the two particular study area. Datasets in tab delimited format are presented here while the reports are been presented in a separate metadata entry. ¶  ¶  ¶  ¶  Dataset was collected using an Echotrac single beam 200-kHz sounder with DGPS for navigation, control based on UTM Zone 4 WGS84 Spheroid. For tide corrections, data retrieved from the gauge sited in Avatiu Harbour was used to reduce all soundings for the harbours surveyed on Rarotonga. All data has been reduced to tide gauge zero with is reported to be 0.02 m above Lowest Astronomical Tide 92-93 as established by the National Tidal Facility Project. These bathymetry dataset are held at the SOPAC Secretariat on behalf of the Cook Islands Goverment. Dataset can be downloaded from the appropriate link under Distribution Infor with appropriate permission from the Secretariat. Bathymetric maps of the four harbours in the Cook Islands, For the Arutanga Harbour, Aitutaki, tidal reduction of soundings was based on measured data from a triton water level gauge.
